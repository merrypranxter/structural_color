# Glossary

## A

**Absorption** - The process by which light energy is converted to other forms (usually heat) when passing through a material.

**Angular Dispersion** - The rate at which different wavelengths are separated by a diffraction grating or prism.

**Anisotropic** - Having different optical properties in different directions. Birefringent materials are anisotropic.

**Aperiodic** - Lacking translational periodicity. Quasicrystals are aperiodic but have long-range order.

## B

**Band Gap** - A range of wavelengths (or frequencies) that cannot propagate through a photonic crystal.

**Birefringence** - The property of having different refractive indices for different polarizations of light.

**Bragg Reflection** - Constructive interference from periodic structures, described by Bragg's law: λ = 2nd sin(θ).

**Bragg Stack** - A multilayer structure that produces Bragg reflection, common in beetle shells.

## C

**Chiral** - Having a handedness (left or right). Cholesteric liquid crystals are chiral.

**Cholesteric** - A type of liquid crystal with a helical structure that produces selective reflection.

**Chromatic Aberration** - The variation of refractive index with wavelength, causing different colors to focus at different points.

**CIE** - Commission Internationale de l'Éclairage, the international authority on light and color standards.

**Constructive Interference** - When waves combine to produce a larger amplitude, occurring when the path difference is an integer multiple of the wavelength.

**Cosine Palette** - A procedural color generation method using cosine functions: palette(t) = a + b·cos(2π(c·t + d)).

## D

**Destructive Interference** - When waves combine to produce a smaller amplitude, occurring when the path difference is a half-integer multiple of the wavelength.

**Diffraction** - The bending of waves around obstacles or through apertures.

**Diffraction Grating** - A periodic structure that separates light into its component wavelengths.

**Dispersion** - The variation of refractive index with wavelength.

## E

**Effective Medium** - A theoretical homogeneous material that approximates the optical properties of a composite or structured material.

**Evanescent Wave** - A wave that decays exponentially from an interface and does not propagate.

**Extinction Coefficient** - The imaginary part of the complex refractive index, describing absorption.

## F

**Fabry-Pérot Interferometer** - An optical cavity formed by two parallel reflective surfaces, producing sharp interference fringes.

**Fermat's Principle** - Light travels along the path that takes the least time.

**Fresnel Equations** - Describe the reflection and transmission of light at an interface.

**FBM (Fractional Brownian Motion)** - A type of noise with controlled roughness, useful for organic textures.

## G

**Gyroid** - A triply periodic minimal surface with interesting photonic properties.

## H

**Helical Pitch** - The distance over which a cholesteric liquid crystal completes one full rotation.

**Huygens' Principle** - Every point on a wavefront can be considered a source of secondary wavelets.

## I

**Index of Refraction (IOR)** - The ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to its speed in a medium.

**Interference** - The combination of waves resulting in a new wave pattern.

**Iridescence** - The phenomenon where color changes with viewing angle due to interference.

## L

**Lamella** - A thin plate or layer. Morpho butterfly wings have lamellae that create structural color.

**Liquid Crystal** - A state of matter with properties between liquids and crystals.

## M

**Maxwell's Equations** - The fundamental equations describing electromagnetic fields and waves.

**Michel-Lévy Chart** - A chart showing interference colors as a function of birefringence and thickness.

**Minimal Surface** - A surface with zero mean curvature, like the gyroid.

**Morpho** - A genus of butterflies famous for their brilliant blue structural color.

## N

**Nanostructure** - A structure with features on the nanometer scale (1-100 nm).

**Newton's Rings** - Circular interference fringes formed between a spherical surface and a flat surface.

**Noise (in shaders)** - Pseudo-random functions used to create organic variation.

## O

**Opalescence** - The milky, iridescent appearance of opals due to light scattering.

**Optical Path Difference (OPD)** - The difference in optical path length between two waves.

**Optical Path Length** - The physical distance multiplied by the refractive index.

**Order (of interference)** - The integer m in interference equations, representing the number of wavelengths in the path difference.

## P

**Phase** - The position of a point in time on a waveform cycle.

**Phase Shift** - A change in the phase of a wave, often occurring at reflection.

**Photonic Crystal** - A periodic dielectric structure that affects the motion of photons.

**Pigment** - A material that absorbs certain wavelengths and reflects others, producing color by subtraction.

**Polarization** - The orientation of the electric field oscillations in light.

## Q

**Quasicrystal** - A structure with long-range order but no periodicity, often with non-crystallographic symmetry (e.g., 5-fold).

## R

**Rayleigh Scattering** - Scattering by particles much smaller than the wavelength, with intensity proportional to 1/λ⁴.

**Reflectance** - The fraction of incident light that is reflected.

**Refractive Index** - See "Index of Refraction".

## S

**Scattering** - The redirection of light by particles or inhomogeneities.

**SDF (Signed Distance Function)** - A function that returns the distance to a surface, negative inside and positive outside.

**Simplex Noise** - A gradient noise function similar to Perlin noise but with fewer directional artifacts.

**Snell's Law** - n₁ sin(θ₁) = n₂ sin(θ₂), describing refraction at an interface.

**Spectral Color** - Color produced by a single wavelength (monochromatic light).

**Structural Color** - Color produced by nanostructure-mediated light interference rather than pigments.

## T

**Thin-Film Interference** - Interference between light reflected from the top and bottom surfaces of a thin film.

**Transfer Matrix Method** - A technique for calculating light propagation through layered structures.

**Transmittance** - The fraction of incident light that is transmitted through a material.

## U

**Uniform (in GLSL)** - A shader variable that has the same value for all vertices/fragments in a draw call.

## V

**Varying (in GLSL)** - A shader variable that is interpolated between vertices.

**View Angle** - The angle between the surface normal and the viewing direction.

**Volumetric Scattering** - Scattering that occurs throughout a volume, not just at surfaces.

## W

**Wavefront** - A surface of constant phase in a wave.

**Wavelength** - The spatial period of a wave, typically measured in nanometers for light.

**Wavenumber** - The reciprocal of wavelength, often measured in cm⁻¹.

**White Light** - Light containing all visible wavelengths in roughly equal proportions.

## X

**X-ray Diffraction** - Diffraction of X-rays by crystals, used to determine atomic structure.

## Y

**Young's Double Slit** - An experiment demonstrating the wave nature of light through interference.

## Z

**Zenith** - The point directly overhead; used in atmospheric optics.
